Welcome Guide 
 
Thank you for choosing Town Square Fort Mill. We are honored to provide an interactive, meaningful, and thoughtful 
experience for your loved one. This guide is designed to give you an overview of our center, the services we provide, and 
the policies and procedures that help us create a safe and engaging environment. 
 
Center Information 
Town Square Fort Mill is open Monday through Friday from 8:30 a.m. to 4:30 p.m. We observe several holiday closures, 
including: 
• New Year’s Day 
• Memorial Day 
• Independence Day (July 4th) 
• Labor Day 
• Thanksgiving Day, with early closure the day before Thanksgiving 
• Christmas Day, with early closure on Christmas Eve 
• New Year’s Eve (early closure) 
 
 
What’s Included Each Day 
Each day at Town Square includes a continental breakfast, a hot lunch, and an afternoon snack. In addition, our team 
provides medication management, toileting and hygiene support, and nursing oversight by licensed professionals. 
Members participate in enriching, structured programs tailored to their cognitive and physical needs, all facilitated by 
compassionate and certified staff. 
 
Billing and Payments 
Billing is processed on the first of each month, or the next business day if the first falls on a weekend or holiday. Families 
receive an invoice for the upcoming month’s membership package as well as any additional services requested. Payment 
is due upon invoicing. 
 
Cancellations and Make-Up Policy 
If a member is unable to attend a scheduled day, we ask that you notify the center at least forty-eight hours in advance 
whenever possible. If less notice is provided, the day will still be charged, but make-up days may be scheduled. These 
must be used within thirty calendar days on a non-regularly scheduled day. 
 
Drop-In Policy 
Drop-in days are only available to current members, as all attendees must be fully registered and have completed 
physician’s forms. Families interested in arranging a drop-in day should contact the Center Director or Coordinator at least 
one business day in advance. 
 
Transportation Services 
Transportation is available from prearranged locations for $10 each way, or door-to-door pick-up for $20 each way. Town 
Square also works with contracted vendors to help families arrange additional transportation solutions as needed. 
Extra Services 
Members may access additional services for an extra fee. Shower assistance is available for $25 per shower, with families 
asked to provide clothing and personal care items in advance. Laundry services are available for $5 per load. Barber and 
salon services may be arranged directly by contacting our stylist at 317-617-3021. For late pick-ups, families are charged 
$25 for the first fifteen minutes, and $2 for each additional minute thereafter, not to exceed thirty minutes.

Additional Care Options 
Town Square partners with licensed third-party providers to make additional services available to families. These may 
include primary care, psychiatric care, and physical, occupational, or speech therapy. These services are not included in 
membership and must be arranged directly with providers, though our staff will gladly assist in coordination. 
 
Communication 
We are committed to open communication with families. Each month, members receive a calendar of activities and a 
lunch menu. Other notices or reminders may be sent home or electronically. Communication specific to an individual 
member is handled directly with their primary contact by the Center Director or Nurse. Families are encouraged to review 
reminders to help members fully participate in themed events and celebrations. For questions or updates, please contact 
us at 803-591-9898 or mpiscatelli@townsquare.net. 
 
Inclement Weather and Emergencies 
The safety of our members and staff is always our highest priority. Transportation providers make their own service 
decisions during inclement weather and will notify families directly of changes. If the center closes due to weather or 
another emergency, families will be notified by email, and closure information may also be posted on our Facebook page. 
If an early closure becomes necessary, families will be contacted directly by a staff member. In the event of an evacuation, 
Town Square will coordinate an alternate location and communicate it immediately to responsible parties. 
 
At Town Square Fort Mill, our commitment is to provide excellent care, engaging programs, and peace of mind to families. 
We look forward to welcoming your loved one into our Town Square family.

Notice of Privacy Practices 
 
MEMBER INFORMATION. MEMBER RIGHTS. OUR RESPONSIBILITIES. 
 
This notice describes how medical information about the Member may be used and disclosed and how the Member (or 
your health care attorney-in-fact) can get access to this information. For purposes of this notice, Member means the 
person attending our Center and receiving the adult day care services. Please review this Notice carefully.  
  
MEMBER RIGHTS  
When it comes to the Member's health information, the Member has certain rights. This section explains the Member's 
rights and some of our responsibilities to help the Member.  
GET AN ELECTRONIC OR PAPER COPY OF THE MEMBER'S MEDICAL RECORD  
• The Member (or their attorney-in-fact) can ask to see or get an electronic or paper copy of the Member's medical 
record and other health information we have about the Member. Ask us how to do this.  
• We will provide a copy or a summary of the Member's health information, usually within 30 days of the request. 
We may charge a reasonable, cost-based fee.  
ASK US TO CORRECT THE MEMBER'S MEDICAL RECORD  
• The Member (or their attorney-in-fact) can ask us to correct health information about the Member that is 
incorrect or incomplete. Ask us how to do this.  
• We may say "no" to the request, but we'll explain why in writing within 60 days.  
REQUEST CONFIDENTIAL COMMUNICATIONS  
• The Member (or their attorney-in-fact) can ask us to contact the Member (or their attorney-in tact) in a specific 
way (for example, home or office phone) or to send mail to a different address.  
• We will say "yes" to all reasonable requests.  
ASK US TO LIMIT WHAT WE USE OR SHARE  
• The Member (or their attorney-in-fact) can ask us not to use or share certain health information for treatment, 
payment, or our operations. We are not required to agree to the request, and we may say "no" if it would affect 
the Member's care.  
• If the Member or representative pays for a service or health care item out-of-pocket in full,  
the Member or representative can ask us not to share that information for the purpose of payment or our operations with 
the Member's health insurer. We will say "yes" unless a law requires us to share that information.  
GET A LIST OF THOSE WITH WHOM WE HAVE SHARED INFORMATION 
• The Member (or their attorney-in-fact) can request a list (accounting) of the times we've shared the Member's 
health information for six years prior to the date requested, who we shared it with, and why.  
• We will include all the disclosures except for those about treatment, payment, and health care  
operations, and certain other disclosures (such as any the Member (or their attorney-in-fact) asked us to make). We'll 
provide one accounting a year for free but will charge a reasonable, cost based fee if the Member (or its attorney-in-fact) 
requests another one within 12 months.  
GET A COPY OF THIS PRIVACY NOTICE  
• The Member (or their attorney-in-fact) can ask for a paper copy of this notice at any time, even if the Member (or 
their attorney-in-fact) has agreed to receive the notice electronically. We will provide a paper copy promptly.  
CHOOSE SOMEONE TO ACT FOR THE MEMBER  
• If the Member has given someone medical power of attorney or if someone is the Member's legal guardian, that 
person can exercise the Member's rights and make choices about the Member's health information.  
• We will make sure the person has this authority and can act for the Member before we take any  
action.

Statement of Rights of Adult Day Care Participants 
  
A. Each Participant must be accorded the following rights:  
o The right to be treated as an Adult, with consideration, respect, and dignity, including privacy in treatment 
and in care for personal needs;  
o The right to participate in a program of services and activities designed to encourage independence, learning, 
growth, and awareness of constructive ways to develop one’s interests and talents;  
o The right to self-determination within the day care setting, including the opportunity to:  
  
 Participate in developing one’s plan for services and any changes therein;  
 Decide whether to participate in any given activity;  
 Be involved to the extent possible in program planning and operation;  
 Refuse treatment, if applicable, and be informed of the consequences of such refusal; and  
 End participation in the Facility any time;  
  
o The right to be cared about in an atmosphere of sincere interest and concern in which needed support and 
services are provided;  
o The right to a safe, secure, and clean environment;  
o The right to confidentiality and the requirement for written consent for release of information to persons not 
authorized under law to receive it;  
o The right to voice grievances without discrimination or reprisal with respect to care or treatment, if 
applicable, that is or is not provided;  
o The right to be fully informed, as evidenced by the Participant’s written acknowledgment of these rights, of all 
rules and regulations regarding Participant conduct and responsibilities;  
o The right to be free from Harm, Exploitation, Abuse, or Neglect;  
o The right to be fully informed, at the time of enrollment, of services and activities available and related 
charges;   
o The right to communicate with others and be understood by them to the extent of the Participant’s 
capability.
